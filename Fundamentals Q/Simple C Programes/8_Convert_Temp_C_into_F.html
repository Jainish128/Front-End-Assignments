The two most often used temperature scales are as follows:
Fahrenheit
Celsius

Celsius vs. Fahrenheit

Celsius is the world’s most widely used temperature scale, commonly known as Centigrade and written as °C.
Celsius is most widely used in scientific and medical applications because it is simpler to work with.
Fahrenheit (written as °F) is still commonly used in many everyday applications in the United States.

Formula : F=(C×9/5)+32
